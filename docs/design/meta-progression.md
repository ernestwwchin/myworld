# Meta-Progression & Town Hub

The Town Hub is the permanent anchor of the game. It is where the player turns short-term dungeon gains into long-term power.

## 1. The Ancient Temple (Soul Mastery)
The source of the player's summoning. This is where **Soul Capacity** is managed.

### Upgrades (Spent with Gold & Ancient Shards)
*   **Soul Breadth:** Unlock the 2nd and 3rd Soul Slots. (Starts with 1).
*   **Soul Depth:** Permanently unlock the ability to conduct **Tier 2** and **Tier 3** Core abilities. 
    *   *Note:* Even if you have a Tier 3 gem, you still need this meta-upgrade to "channel" that much power.
*   **Soul Recovery:** Buy "Tethers" that allow you to keep your gems automatically on one death (skipping the Corpse Run).

---

## 2. The Blacksmith (Blueprint Refinement)
The Blacksmith doesn't forge individual swords; they **refine the blueprints** of the world.

### Blueprint Upgrades
When a player finds a Blueprint (e.g. "Steel Longsword"), the Blacksmith can upgrade it:
*   **Level 1 (Base):** Item can drop in the dungeon.
*   **Level 2:** Items of this type found in the dungeon gain a **+1 Slot Level** bonus (making them better conduits for Soul Cores).
*   **Level 3:** Items of this type gain a **Unique Passive** (e.g. "Steel Longswords now have +10% Crit Chance").

---

## 3. The Blueprint Pool
The game starts with a very small loot pool (Rusty Dagger, Rags).
*   **Discovery:** Finding a Blueprint in a deep dungeon floor "pollutes" the global loot pool with that item.
*   **Rewarding Loop:** Every successful run makes the *next* run more interesting because there are more (and better) items to find.

---

## 4. The Library (Lore & NPCs)
Rescuing NPCs from the dungeon (like the "Prison Rescue" scenario) brings them back to Town.
*   **Rescued NPCs** provide new services:
    *   *The Alchemist:* Sells better starting potions.
    *   *The Cartographer:* Shows more of the minimap at the start of a run.
    *   *The Lorekeeper:* Unlocks permanent backstory entries about the Ancient Temple.
